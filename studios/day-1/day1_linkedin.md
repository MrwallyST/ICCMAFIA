## 3. Core Market Structures: The "Dribbling" Fundamentals
I don't care about basketball, but I know you have to learn how to dribble before you start doing crossovers and hizzys. In trading, "dribbling" is market structure.

### The Three States of Market Movement
1.  **Uptrends:** Price is climbing.
2.  **Downtrends:** Price is falling.
3.  **Consolidation:** Price is moving sideways and breaking all the rules.

### Defining Price Points
You identify trends by looking at two things: Highs and Lows. These are your pivot points.

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **High** | The highest point price reaches before it **pivoted** and started declining. |
| **Low** | The lowest point price reaches before it **pivoted** and started increasing. |

---

## 4. Technical Analysis: Identifying Trends

### The Downtrend Hierarchy
A downtrend is a series of lower levels. Drill this into your head:
*   **Lower Low (LL):** This happens when price **breaks the previous low**. It is literally lower than the previous low point.
*   **Lower High (LH):** A peak that is made, but it fails to get as high as the previous peak.

### The Uptrend Hierarchy
*   **Higher High (HH):** Price pushes past the previous peak. 
    *   *The Analogy:* It’s like smoking weed or doing drugs. You hit a high and think, "This is the highest I can get." Then you do something stronger and you get even higher. Life just keeps getting higher and higher—that is a Higher High.
*   **Higher Low (HL):** Price drops, but the new low point is **higher than the previous low**. This shows the market is still pushing up.

### Structural Integrity
Price will not break these levels on purpose on the higher time frames (like the 4-hour or 1-hour). These structures are strong. Price only breaks them when it intends to reverse or start consolidating. Stay on the higher time frames; it’s the fastest way to start making real money.

## 5. The "No-Trade" Zone: Understanding Consolidation

### Defining Consolidation
Consolidation is when the market has no rules. It breaks the high, then it breaks the low, and it just moves sideways. 

### The "Criminal Friend" Analogy
Trading mirrors life. You wouldn’t hang out with criminals because they break the rules. So why would you put your hard-earned money into a market that is constantly breaking the rules of structure? If price isn't following the "Uptrend" or "Downtrend" rules, it’s a criminal. Stay away from it.

### Identification and Action
*   **Red Flags:** You see a high, then a low, then a "higher high" that immediately crashes back down to break the low. It’s sideways and messy.
*   **Action:** If it’s breaking rules, don't associate with it.

> **WARNING:** Do not trade consolidation. If price is not following clear structural rules, you stay out.

## 6. Practical Application: The NASDAQ Example

### Chart Analysis
I’ve traded the NASDAQ for four years and made millions. I prefer the 4-hour time frame because the structure is clear and reliable.

### Anatomy of a Trend Shift (4-Hour Walkthrough)
When price shifts into a downtrend, you can track it chronologically:
1.  **The High:** Price reaches a peak.
2.  **The Low:** Price drops to a point and **pivots**.
3.  **The Lower High (LH):** Price moves up but fails to beat the previous High. It stays lower.
4.  **The Shift:** Price reaches a point before making a shift in another direction.
5.  **The Lower Low (LL):** Price drops and breaks below the previous Low, confirming the trend is now down.
