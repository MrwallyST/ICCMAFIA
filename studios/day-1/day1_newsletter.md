## 4. Market Structure: The Three States of Price
Price only does three things: it goes up, it goes down, or it goes sideways. You must be able to identify these states instantly.

| Term | Definition based on Source Context |
| :--- | :--- |
| **High** | The highest point price reaches before it **pivots** downward. |
| **Low** | The lowest point price reaches after a decline before it **pivots** upward. |
| **Higher High (HH)** | A point where price breaks above the previous high and **pivots** at a new peak. |
| **Higher Low (HL)** | A pullback that **pivots** at a point higher than the previous low. |
| **Lower Low (LL)** | A point where price drops below the previous low and **pivots** at a new bottom. |
| **Lower High (LH)** | A bounce that **pivots** at a point lower than the previous high. |

## 5. Anatomy of an Uptrend vs. Downtrend
A trend is just a sequence of pivots that follow a specific set of rules.

### Identifying an Uptrend
1.  **The High and the Low:** Identify your starting boundaries.
2.  **The Higher High (HH):** Price must break above the initial high. 
    *   *The Analogy:* Think of it like drugs. Smoking weed sets a high, but doing cocaine sets a "higher high." Life just gets higher and higher.
3.  **The Higher Low (HL):** Price pulls back but stays above the previous low point.
4.  **Confirmation:** The sequence of HH and HL repeats.

### Identifying a Downtrend
1.  **The High and the Low:** Establish your initial pivots.
2.  **The Lower High (LH):** Price bounces but fails to reach the previous high.
3.  **The Lower Low (LL):** Price drops and breaks below the previous low.
4.  **Confirmation:** The sequence of LH and LL repeats as price declines.

## 7. Practical Chart Analysis: The 4-Hour Example
When you look at a real 4-hour chart, the price won't always look like a perfect drawing. You must track the logic of the pivots:
*   **Start at the High:** Identify the peak of the move.
*   **Find the Low:** Look for where price first pivoted up.
*   **Watch the LH (Lower High):** If the next bounce fails to break that peak, mark it as an LH.
*   **Confirm the LL (Lower Low):** When price pushes down and **breaks the established support** of the previous low, you have your LL.

By tracking these points all the way down, you ensure you are on the right side of the market's momentum.
