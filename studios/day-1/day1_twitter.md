## 4. The Three Market States: Fundamentals of Trend Identification
You need to learn how to "dribble" before you start trying to do crossovers, hizzys, and shazams. In trading, "dribbling" is identifying the three market states: Uptrends, Downtrends, and Consolidation.

### The Downtrend (The "Dribbling" Phase)
A downtrend consists of a series of **Lower Highs (LH)** and **Lower Lows (LL)**. 
*   **The Pivot:** A point only becomes a "Low" or a "High" because price reaches a certain level of decline or increase and then **pivots** (changes direction).
*   **The Confirmation:** 
    1.  Price reaches a point and pivots up = **Low**.
    2.  Price moves back down and breaks below that previous low = **Lower Low (LL)**.
    3.  Price retraces upward but pivots back down before reaching the original high = **Lower High (LH)**.

### The Uptrend
An uptrend is defined by **Higher Highs (HH)** and **Higher Lows (HL)**. Think of it like this: you hit the "highest point" you think is possible, but then life moves higher. Price expands, hits a peak, pulls back to form a **Higher Low (HL)**, and then breaks the previous peak to create a new **Higher High (HH)**. It is a constant cycle of expansion.

### Consolidation (The "No-Trade" Zone)
Consolidation is sideways movement. This is where price breaks its own rules—it breaks the **HH** and then breaks the **LL** immediately after. It has no direction.
*   **The Criminal Analogy:** Trading and life mirror each other. You wouldn't hang out with criminals because they break the rules of society. If the market is breaking its own rules (consolidation), why would you put your money there?
*   **Instruction:** **Do not trade consolidation.** If the market is not following a clear structure, stay away from it.

## 5. Technical Analysis Example: NASDAQ Case Study
Let’s look at the NASDAQ (NAS100) on a **4-hour chart**. I’ve traded this for four years and made millions on it because I follow the structure.

In a trending market, you can track the price all the way down by identifying the shifts:
1.  **High:** The starting peak.
2.  **Low:** The first point price **pivots** upward.
3.  **Lower High (LH):** The next peak that fails to break the previous High.
4.  **Lower Low (LL):** The point where price breaks the previous Low. (Note: These **LL** points often act as **Support**, which we will discuss in a later lesson).

By identifying this sequence—High, Low, **Lower High (LH)**, **Lower Low (LL)**—you are proving that the trend is following the rules.
