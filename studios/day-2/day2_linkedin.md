# Day 2: ICC Framework Decoded Part 2 - Support, Resistance & Indication

### 1. The Concept of Indication as a Trend Catalyst

In the ICC Framework, we do not guess where the market is going; we wait for the market to tell us. This "telling" is what we call an Indication. You must understand that Indication is strictly **information**—it is the blueprint that dictates your entire trade plan before you ever risk a dollar.

> **DEFINITION: Indication occurs the moment price breaks above a specific Swing High or below a specific Swing Low. It is the definitive signal that a trend is either starting or resuming in that direction.**

Indication is the logical answer to the two most critical questions every trader must ask:
*   **What is the trend?** The Indication reveals the market's current intent. If price breaks a high, the trend is bullish; if it breaks a low, it is bearish.
*   **Where is price headed?** The momentum that causes the break establishes the trajectory and the eventual price target.

---

### 2. Defining the Foundations: Swing Highs and Swing Lows

To identify an Indication, you must first master the identification of Swings. These levels act as the functional support and resistance in our framework.

| Term | Definition / Characteristic |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Swing High** | A peak where price reaches its highest point, rejects that level, and reverses direction. It serves as established resistance. |
| **Swing Low** | A valley where price reaches a point of decline, rejects that level, and reverses upward. It serves as established support. |
| **Pivots** | The absolute turning point where price reaches a specific increase or decrease and "bends." This is the precise vertex where the shift in momentum occurs. |
| **Swings (General)** | The structural building blocks of the market (Higher Highs, Higher Lows, Lower Highs, Lower Lows). |

---

### 3. The Mechanics of Support & Resistance Breaks

You must recognize that price movement is not random; it is strictly logic-based. For an Indication to be valid, price must prove it has the momentum to clear established swing levels. 

The logic follows this specific sequence:

1.  **Consolidation:** Price "equals out" between a swing high and a swing low. This is the danger zone where no clear trend exists.
2.  **The Break (Validation of Momentum):** Price gains sufficient volume to push through a swing level. Consider the **500 level** as an example. If resistance is at 500 and price breaks above it, that break *validates* the momentum. The same strength that drove price through 500 is now confirmed to have the potential to carry it to the **600 level**.
3.  **The Target Shift:** Once the level is broken, the "Information" is revealed. The market has signaled its intent to move toward the next price objective, transitioning from a stagnant range into a high-probability trend.

---

### 4. Market Dynamics: Liquidity and the "Breakout Trap"

One of the most common mistakes developing traders make is entering the market the moment they see a breakout. I am telling you now: **Do not trade the Indication.**

> **WARNING: THE BREAKOUT TRAP**
> 
> When price makes a new high or low (the Indication), it often triggers a "liquidity grab" or "shakeout." The market is actively looking for breakout traders to liquidate. If the market sees a cluster of buy orders on a breakout, it will often make a subtle, aggressive movement in the opposite direction to wipe out that money and hit stop-losses before the real move begins. This is why we wait for the "second go-round"—the correction—where the entry is significantly safer.

---

### 5. Tactical Framework: Timeframes and Analysis

To find clear indications and reliable swing levels, you must utilize the correct timeframe hierarchy.

*   ### Primary Timeframe: 1-Hour
    The 1-Hour chart is our standard. It is where I personally identify trends and swings because it provides the best balance of structural strength and tradeable volume. 
*   ### Clarity Filter: 4-Hour
    If the 1-Hour chart looks "noisy" or unclear, scale up to the 4-Hour timeframe. The 4-Hour serves as a filter to see the broader market structure and confirm the true direction of the momentum.
*   ### Refinement Timeframe: 30-Minute
    The 30-Minute chart is used to identify subtle supports and granular price action. While useful for refining your view, it should only be used once the 1-Hour or 4-Hour trend has been established.

---

### 6. From Indication to Execution: The Blueprint Approach

Repeat this mantra: **Indication is Information.** It is never an entry signal. It provides the tactical foresight to know where your entry and take-profit will be before the market even offers the opportunity.

The ICC Information Logic follows this mandatory loop:
1.  **Indication (The Blueprint):** Price breaks a swing level. We now have the "Information" regarding direction and targets.
2.  **Correction (The Test):** Price pulls back to re-test the market structure. This phase "shakes out" the weak hands and validates the new support/resistance.
3.  **Continuation (The Execution):** Once the correction respects the new structure, we execute the trade, following the momentum confirmed by the initial Indication.

### 7. Summary of Key Fundamentals

Success in this framework requires you to master the "dribbling" of the market. You would never attempt a fadeaway three-pointer if you haven't mastered the basic handle; similarly, you cannot execute high-level trades if you cannot identify a basic trend.

Everything in the market follows rules—Fundamentals, Technicals, and Structure. If the market is not following the rules, you must have the discipline to walk away. Trading only when the market respects these structural laws is the only path to consistency.

**The Lead Educator’s Rule:** Do not trade consolidation; it is the enemy of capital. Stay patient, wait for the Indication, and follow the blueprint.
