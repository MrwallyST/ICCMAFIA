# Day 2 Study Guide: ICC Framework Decoded — Indications, Swings & Market Structure

This study guide covers the core concepts from **Day 2** of the TradesBySci ICC course: how to identify swing highs and swing lows, understand the Indication concept, recognize liquidity traps, and apply the 3-Phase ICC Blueprint (Indication → Correction → Continuation).

---

## I. Swing Highs and Swing Lows — The Building Blocks of Trend

A **Swing High** is the highest point price reaches before it reverses and heads lower.
A **Swing Low** is the lowest point price reaches before it pivots and heads higher.

These pivot points are the foundation of all market structure analysis on the **1H and 4H timeframes**, which TradesBySci uses as the primary reference frames.

### Trend Identification Using Swings

| Trend Type | Swing Pattern | Action |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Uptrend** | Higher Highs (HH) + Higher Lows (HL) | Look for buys |
| **Downtrend** | Lower Highs (LH) + Lower Lows (LL) | Look for sells |
| **Consolidation** | No clear swing pattern | Stay out |

**Key Rule:** If price breaks the structure (e.g., breaks a Higher Low in an uptrend), the trend is either reversing or consolidating — stay away.

---

## II. The Indication — When a Trend Begins

An **Indication** occurs when price **breaks above a Swing High** (bullish) or **breaks below a Swing Low** (bearish). This is the official signal that a new trend is attempting to begin.

- An Indication is **not** an entry signal — it is a **blueprint**.
- It tells you: *What is the trend direction?* and *Where is price potentially headed?*
- TradesBySci uses the 1H timeframe primarily; the 4H when the 1H is unclear.

### Why the Indication Is NOT an Entry

After price makes a new high (Indication), the market frequently reverses sharply to **grab liquidity** — wiping out traders who entered on the breakout. This is called a **Liquidity Grab** or **Fakeout**.

---

## III. The 3-Phase ICC Blueprint

| Phase | What Happens | Trader Action |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Indication (I)** | Price breaks above a Swing High or below a Swing Low | Identify the direction and target |
| **Correction (C)** | Price pulls back after the indication — liquidity grab | Wait here for the entry |
| **Continuation (C)** | Price resumes the original direction | Execute the trade |

### The Power of "The Second Time Around"
A fakeout breaks the level once. A **real move** will retest the level (Correction) and then push through again (Continuation). Waiting for this second push significantly reduces the chance of being liquidated.

---

## IV. Liquidity and Market Manipulation

After a new high is made, the market often creates a **subtle move in the opposite direction** specifically to remove traders who entered on the breakout. TradesBySci calls this a **Liquidity Grab**.

- **Fakeout:** Price breaks a level but immediately returns — no follow-through.
- **Real Move:** Price breaks, corrects, then continues in the original direction.

**Rule:** View every Indication as information, never as an immediate entry.

## VI. Key Terms Glossary

- **Swing High:** The highest price point before a reversal lower.
- **Swing Low:** The lowest price point before a reversal higher.
- **Indication:** Price breaking above a Swing High or below a Swing Low, signaling a new trend.
- **Correction:** The pullback/retracement that follows an Indication; used to grab liquidity.
- **Continuation:** The resumption of the trend after the Correction — the entry zone.
- **Liquidity Grab:** A sharp, short-lived move against the trend direction to wipe out breakout traders.
- **Fakeout:** An Indication that fails — price breaks the level but reverses instead of continuing.
- **Higher High (HH):** A peak higher than the previous peak — sign of an uptrend.
- **Higher Low (HL):** A valley higher than the previous valley — sign of an uptrend.
- **Lower High (LH):** A peak lower than the previous peak — sign of a downtrend.
- **Lower Low (LL):** A valley lower than the previous valley — sign of a downtrend.
- **ICC Method:** Indication → Correction → Continuation — the 3-phase trade entry framework.
