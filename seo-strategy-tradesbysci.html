Key Findings

The TradesBySci site is heavily reliant on JavaScript to render its course content (days.json). Search engine crawlers evaluate the raw HTML, which currently contains very little text regarding "price action", "market structure", or "ICC trading". Furthermore, foundational SEO infrastructure like a robots.txt file, sitemap.xml, Open Graph tags, and structured data are completely missing. This makes organic discovery through Google search highly improbable in its current state.

YouTube Strategy & Monetization
Based on your specific questions during intake
1. One Channel vs. Two Channels (Bilingual Strategy)

The 2026 Recommendation: Keep it on ONE channel utilizing YouTube's Multi-Language Audio (MLA).

  • Why? The YouTube algorithm rewards "velocity" and total watch time. Splitting your audience into two channels means you start from zero twice.
  • How it works: You upload the video once. You add the English audio track, and then upload the Spanish `.mp3` as an alternate audio track on the same video. YouTube automatically plays the correct language based on the viewer's location/preferences.
  • Action item: Ensure your video titles and descriptions are localized (translated natively via YouTube's subtitle/metadata tab).
2. How to Monetize a "Free" Course

Giving the course away for free is an excellent "Top of Funnel" strategy. Here is how trading educators typically monetize the back-end without charging for the curriculum:

  • Affiliate Broker Links: Recommend the broker you use to trade futures (e.g., NinjaTrader, Tradeovate, Topstep, Apex Funding). Prop firms pay massive commissions for referrals.
  • Charting Software: Put a TradingView affiliate link in every video description and on the website.
  • Private Community (Discord/Skool): The course is free to learn, but being in a room with you to discuss live market structure and daily setups costs $30 - $50/month.
  • 1-on-1 Mentorship: A small percentage of viewers will want you to review their journal. You already built the Journal app — charge for your time to review their logs.
